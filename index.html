Public Transport Delay Analysis

Urban Mobility · Operational Efficiency · Root-Cause Analysis

PythonPandasSeabornSQLPower BI

Problem Statement

A city transport authority was experiencing rising passenger dissatisfaction with no clear data-driven view of where, when, and why delays were concentrated. The goal: diagnose systemic bottlenecks and recommend operational changes projected to reduce average delay by 15%.

Dataset

120K+ trip records across 40 bus routes, 6 months — including departure times, scheduled vs. actual arrival, route ID, weather flag, and peak/off-peak tags.

Approach

  • Cleaned 8% missing/malformed timestamps; standardized route ID formats across 3 data sources
  • Engineered delay severity tiers (0–5 min, 5–15 min, 15+ min) for risk segmentation
  • Time-series analysis across hour-of-day and day-of-week to surface delay seasonality
  • Chi-square test to quantify weather flag vs. delay magnitude correlation

Key Insights

  • Routes 12 and 27 accounted for 34% of all severe delays while serving only 18% of passengers
  • Delays on rainy days were 2.4× higher on routes with non-covered stops
  • Off-peak scheduling gaps of 22+ minutes correlated with next-trip overcrowding and cascading delays

Business Impact

Recommendations: rebalance frequency on Routes 12 & 27 during AM peak, projecting 22% reduction in total delay-minutes. Infrastructure note flagged 6 uncovered stops with the highest rainy-day delay scores.

02

Walmart Sales Performance Analysis

Retail Analytics · Forecasting · Store Benchmarking

PythonScikit-learnSQLExcelPower BI

Problem Statement

With 45 stores and 3 years of weekly sales data, the challenge was identifying underperforming stores against forecast, and determining whether macroeconomic indicators (CPI, fuel price, unemployment) correlated with sales variance.

Dataset

Walmart Kaggle dataset — 421,570 rows, 45 stores, 99 departments, weekly sales figures, holiday flags, temperature, fuel price, CPI, and unemployment rate.

Approach

  • Store and department-level aggregation using Pandas multi-index groupby
  • Linear regression to quantify CPI and unemployment impact on weekly sales (R² = 0.71)
  • Holiday vs. non-holiday sales lift via Welch's t-test — statistically significant result
  • Store efficiency ranking by revenue output and YoY growth rate

Business Impact

Insight package recommending inventory pre-loading for Thanksgiving + Super Bowl windows. Targeted promotions proposed for underperforming stores in high-unemployment zip codes. Regression model achieved R² of 0.71.

Renewable Energy Trends (2000–2023)

Energy Analytics · Policy Intelligence · Geospatial Trends

PythonPlotlyK-MeansPower BIExcel

Problem Statement

As global energy policy debates intensify, stakeholders need a reliable analytical view of renewable energy adoption trajectories by country, source, and decade. This project delivers a 23-year trend analysis designed for energy policy teams and ESG-focused investors.

Dataset

Our World in Data — 200+ countries, 23 years, covering solar, wind, hydro, and bioenergy capacity in GW and as percentage of total energy mix.

Approach

  • Longitudinal trend analysis using rolling averages and CAGR per country-source pair
  • K-Means clustering to classify countries into 4 adoption tiers (early-adopter, growing, laggard, fossil-dependent)
  • Choropleth map built with Plotly Express for 2023 renewable share by country
  • YoY delta analysis to detect policy-shift inflection points

ESG investor briefing deck with country-level opportunity scoring for renewable infrastructure investment. Highest-ROI markets flagged based on policy momentum and current renewable share gap.
